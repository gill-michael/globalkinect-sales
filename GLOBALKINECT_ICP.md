## Structure

GlobalKinect operates two sites serving distinct primary audiences.
The ICPs below are mapped to each site. Do not mix the messaging
of .ae ICPs into .co.uk and vice versa.

**globalkinect.ae** — GCC and regional specialist site
**globalkinect.co.uk** — Global platform site

## THE .AE SITE — GCC AND REGIONAL ICPs

---

### ICP A1 — The Frustrated GCC Operator
**The core .ae audience. Highest priority.**

Who they are:
An HR Director, Finance Director, or senior Operations lead
at a business already running across 2+ GCC countries.
They know exactly how painful multi-country GCC payroll is.
They are not looking to be educated — they are looking for
a way out of the mess they are already in.

Company profile:
- 50–500 employees
- 2–5 GCC countries simultaneously
- UAE or Saudi Arabia as primary HQ
- Sectors: engineering, construction, hospitality, logistics,
  healthcare, retail, real estate, professional services
- Current setup: different bureau per country, monthly
  spreadsheet submissions, EOSB tracked manually, WPS
  managed separately, no single HR view across countries

The pain in their words:
"I spend three days every month chasing five different
providers. I have no idea what our total workforce costs
across countries in real time. Saudi and UAE want completely
different things and I'm managing it all manually."

What they need to hear:
Recognition before features. They need to feel that
GlobalKinect understands their specific situation before
they will listen to anything else. Not "here's our platform"
— "we know exactly what you're dealing with."

Hook:
"Still sending spreadsheets to separate bureaus in each
country? One platform. One submission. All of it sorted."

Entry point:
Single-country payroll bureau (UAE or Saudi) is often
the first conversation. They fix the worst country first
then expand. PEPM pricing. Low friction to start.

Sales motion:
Demo showing live payroll run for their specific countries.
EOSB calculation, WPS file generation, multi-country view.
Show them the output they currently spend days producing
— completed in one submission.

### ICP A2 — The GCC Single-Country SME
**High volume. Shorter cycle. .ae only.**

Who they are:
A smaller business in one GCC country — usually UAE or
Saudi Arabia. Running payroll manually or through a local
accountant. Growing fast enough that the manual approach
is breaking. May not know what HRIS means but knows they
need something better.

Company profile:
- 10–100 employees
- Single country — UAE most common, Saudi growing
- Sectors: SME across any sector — retail, F&B, services,
  tech startups, professional services, agencies
- Current setup: accountant does payroll, spreadsheet-tracked,
  employee records in email or basic software
- Likely no dedicated HR function

The pain in their words:
"We have 40 people and payroll is still done in Excel.
Every month something goes wrong. We don't track leave
properly. We have no idea what our EOSB liability is.
We're growing and it's getting out of hand."

What they need to hear:
Simple. Clean. Affordable. Works for one country.
Doesn't need a six-month implementation. Can start now.

Hook:
"UAE payroll sorted. Employee portal included.
One monthly fee per employee. Get started this week."

Entry point:
Single-country payroll bureau. PEPM fee — low barrier,
immediate value. Employee portal (payslips, personal info)
included free with payroll. HRIS and additional countries
available as they grow.

Conversion path:
This buyer will not book a demo without knowing the cost.
The .ae site needs a pricing page or calculator as a
conversion path alongside the demo CTA. Price clarity
is the primary conversion driver for this ICP.

Decision maker:
CEO, COO, or owner directly. Fast decision cycle if
price is clear and onboarding is simple.

### ICP A3 — The Scaling GCC Business
**Direct outbound primary. Vision 2030 driven.**

Who they are:
A Saudi or UAE-headquartered business growing fast.
Has outgrown their current setup — if they had one at
all. CEO or COO is making the call. Often the HR function
is one person or doesn't exist yet.

Company profile:
- 50–300 employees, growing to 500+
- Saudi Arabia (Riyadh) or UAE (Dubai) HQ
- Multi-country GCC presence emerging
- Sectors: technology, fintech, hospitality, construction,
  healthcare, sport and entertainment, logistics
- Often recently funded or winning Vision 2030 contracts

The pain in their words:
"We hired 60 people this year. Payroll is a nightmare.
We don't have a proper HR system. Saudization compliance
is stressing me out. We need to sort this before it
becomes a bigger problem."

What they need to hear:
You're growing fast. Your current setup won't hold.
Fix it now before it breaks.

Hook:
"You're scaling fast. Your payroll setup isn't.
GlobalKinect grows with you."

Entry point:
Payroll bureau plus HRIS together. They want a full
solution, not piecemeal. Often introduced by the
funding signals and direct LinkedIn outbound at the point of a
significant hire.

Sales motion:
Direct outbound via LinkedIn and email. Short demo. Emphasise speed
of setup and Saudization/Emiratisation compliance
built in to the platform.

## THE .CO.UK SITE — GLOBAL PLATFORM ICPs

---

### ICP B1 — The European Multi-Country Employer
**The primary .co.uk audience.**

Who they are:
A business headquartered in the UK, Ireland, Sweden,
Cyprus, or elsewhere in Europe managing employees across
multiple countries. Dealing with the same fragmentation
problem as GCC operators — just in a different geography.
Different bureaus per country, spreadsheets, no single
view. Actively looking to consolidate.

Company profile:
- 100–1,000 employees
- Operations across 2+ European countries
- HQ: UK, Ireland, Sweden, Cyprus, or similar
- Sectors: technology, professional services, financial
  services, media, retail, healthcare, education
- Current setup: fragmented — different payroll providers
  per country, manual reporting, no unified HR system

The pain in their words:
"We have people in six countries and we're running payroll
six different ways. No single view of headcount or cost.
Month-end is a nightmare every time."

What they need to hear:
The same core message as every other ICP — one platform
replacing the fragmented mess — just anchored in their
European geography rather than GCC.

Hook:
"Replace your fragmented country-by-country payroll
setup with one clean platform. 100+ countries. One login."

Entry point:
Full platform conversation. HRIS plus payroll bureau
across their existing countries. Demo shows their
specific country mix running in one place.

Note on local presence:
GlobalKinect offices in UK, Ireland, Sweden, and Cyprus
give this buyer a locally credible entry point. A Swedish
company sees a Swedish presence. An Irish company sees
an Irish presence. Use this actively — do not bury it.

---

### ICP B2 — The UK Domestic Business
**Entry-level. High volume. Upsell path.**

Who they are:
A UK business that needs clean, managed UK payroll on
a platform. May have no international complexity yet.
The simplest possible entry point into GlobalKinect.

Company profile:
- 10–200 employees
- UK only or UK primary
- Any sector
- Current setup: basic payroll software, accountant,
  or manual — looking to professionalise

What they need to hear:
Clean UK payroll. Simple platform. PEPM pricing.
No complexity, no long implementation.

Hook:
"UK payroll sorted. One platform. One monthly fee."

Entry point:
UK payroll bureau. PEPM. Employee portal included.
Natural upsell path: add Ireland, Sweden, or Cyprus
as they open offices. Add EOR as they hire internationally.

Note:
This ICP is the European equivalent of ICP A2 on the
.ae site. Volume play. Fast decision cycle. Price clarity
matters.

### ICP B3 — The International Expander into GCC
**Bridge ICP. Uses both sites.**

Who they are:
A UK or EU-based business expanding into GCC markets
or already operating there. Unfamiliar with GCC
compliance specifics. High anxiety. Willing to pay
for confidence and speed. Often the highest contract
value of any ICP.

Company profile:
- 100–2,000 employees globally
- GCC headcount: 10–200 or planning to hire
- HQ: UK, Germany, Netherlands, Sweden, Ireland
- Sectors: professional services, energy, construction,
  defence, technology, financial services, healthcare
- Often using a generic global provider that handles
  GCC poorly

The pain in their words:
"We won a contract in Saudi and we have no idea how
to get people paid there compliantly. Our current
platform doesn't really handle GCC properly. We need
someone who actually knows this market."

What they need to hear:
GCC specialist credibility. Local presence (Dubai office).
Global platform with exceptional GCC depth. Fast setup.

Hook:
"Your global payroll platform doesn't know the GCC
like we do. We built GlobalKinect here."

Key triggers to watch for:
- Saudi RHQ Programme — companies required to establish
  Riyadh headquarters and run KSA payroll for first time
- New GCC contract win announced
- Senior hire into UAE or Saudi requiring compliant setup
- Acquisition of a GCC-based business

Journey:
Lands on .co.uk first. GCC depth gives them confidence.
Dubai office confirms local presence. Demo shows KSA
and UAE payroll running live. Often becomes a full
platform client across European and GCC countries.

### ICP B4 — The European-GCC Bridge Company
**Highest value. Both sites relevant.**

Who they are:
A company with operations in both Europe and GCC —
the buyer who needs both sides of what GlobalKinect
does. A German engineering firm with Germany, Sweden,
and Saudi operations. A UK professional services firm
with London, Dublin, and Dubai offices. Nobody else
serves this buyer as a single platform.

Why this matters:
This ICP has the highest contract value and the longest
retention because switching costs are highest when
GlobalKinect is running payroll across both their
European and GCC entities. They have no obvious
alternative that does both well.

Hook:
"European payroll and GCC payroll. One platform.
One relationship. Finally."

Sales motion:
Full platform demo showing European countries and GCC
countries simultaneously in one view. The contrast with
their current fragmented setup does the selling.

### ICP B5 — The Platform Integrator
**B2B only. Direct outreach. Not on either website.**

Who they are:
An HRIS or HR tech platform with clients in GCC or
European markets but no payroll engine for those regions.
Wants to embed or white-label GlobalKinect's capability.

This is a completely separate commercial conversation.
Different deck. Different pricing. Different process.
Never mixed into main website or marketing messaging.
No public-facing page on either site.

## Pricing model — consistent across all ICPs

Same platform. Different scope per client.

- PEPM fee — per employee per month, per country
- Employee portal — included free with any payroll service
- Additional modules — HRIS, EOR, more countries on top
- EOR — same PEPM structure per country

Entry is always low friction. One country, one service,
one monthly fee. Expansion is natural — add countries
or modules as the client grows.

For ICPs A2 and B2 (SME single-country):
Lead with the PEPM number on the website. Price clarity
converts this buyer. They will not book a demo without
knowing roughly what it costs.

For all other ICPs:
PEPM discussed in the demo, not on the website.

## .ae site — specific requirements

Local presence signals — prominent, not buried:
- Dubai office address in header or top of footer
- UAE phone number
- "Based in Dubai. Built for the Gulf."

Tone — more direct and regional than .co.uk:
Less "global tech platform". More "we are here,
we know this market, we run this every day."

GCC-specific content:
- UAE WPS compliance — what it is, how the platform handles it
- Saudi EOSB calculation — automatic, built in
- Saudization and Emiratisation tracking
- Country payroll pages for UAE, Saudi, Qatar, Kuwait,
  Bahrain, Oman, Egypt
- Local compliance update content — regulatory changes

Pricing page or calculator:
Required for ICP A2 conversion. PEPM structure.
Clear enough to self-qualify without a demo.

---

## .co.uk site — specific requirements

Office presence — show all locations:
London + Dublin + Stockholm + Nicosia + Dubai
Even if offices are in process — "opening soon" is fine.
The presence of multiple offices signals a real business.

Tone — global platform confidence:
The global story is the headline. GCC depth is the
proof point that differentiates from generic providers.

European country content:
Country pages or sections for UK, Ireland, Sweden,
Cyprus — and all 100+ countries accessible from
the platform. These serve SEO and AEO as much as
direct conversion.

GCC as a differentiator not a headline:
"We're exceptionally good at GCC" is a trust signal
on the .co.uk site — not the leading message.

## What NOT to do across either site

- Do not mix A2 messaging (simple, cheap, one country)
  with B3 messaging (global, complex, compliance-heavy)
  on the same page
- Do not mention partner networks or delivery infrastructure
- Do not position EOR as the headline product on either site
- Do not use the same hero headline on both sites
- Do not bury office locations — they are free credibility
- Do not make the managed service buyer feel like they
  are in the wrong place on a platform-first site
